# 55 Screens in 8 Weeks

Erudio came to me with a problem: they had a product vision, a development team, and no design. The go-to-market date was fixed. Eight weeks to deliver a complete UX system for a personalized learning platform.

This is a story about what happens when constraints are real and the work still has to be good.

## The Problem

Erudio's hypothesis: traditional e-learning platforms (Coursera, Udemy) are built for content delivery. They don't adapt to how the learner is actually doing. Students complete 15% of courses on average not because the content is bad, but because the experience doesn't respond to them.

The product: AI-assisted learning paths that adapt in real-time based on comprehension signals, time-on-task, and self-reported confidence. The UX challenge: making this adaptation visible without being intrusive.

## Research Sprint: Days 1–7

No time for extended fieldwork. Rapid methods:

- Contextual inquiry with 8 existing learners (3 sessions/day for 2 days)
- Heuristic analysis of Duolingo, Notion, and Linear (chosen for adaptive complexity)
- Card sorting with 12 participants for navigation taxonomy
- Expert review from one learning science researcher at the university

Key finding: learners don't want to see AI making decisions. They want to feel like *they're* making decisions, supported by information they trust. The system should be invisible.

> "Design for the feeling of agency, even when the system is guiding."

## Information Architecture

The 55 screens covered:

1. **Onboarding** (7 screens) — goal-setting, skill assessment, schedule preferences
2. **Dashboard** (5 screens) — today's focus, progress overview, recent activity
3. **Course catalog** (6 screens) — discovery, filtering, course preview
4. **Learning session** (12 screens) — video, exercises, comprehension checks, notes
5. **Progress tracking** (8 screens) — skill map, streak, milestone celebrations
6. **Settings & profile** (9 screens) — account, notifications, accessibility
7. **Social/community** (8 screens) — peer study groups, discussion threads

The hardest IA decision: where to put the adaptive path visualization. Every option was either too prominent (creates anxiety) or too buried (invisible, defeats purpose). Final decision: visible on demand via a dedicated "path" tab, not on the dashboard.

## Design System

A full design system was built in parallel — not after. Every component was designed to its token system: spacing scale, color scale, type scale, interaction states.

The component library delivered to engineering: 47 components, fully documented, with all states (default, hover, focus, active, disabled, loading, error).

## Outcome

The MVP launched on the target date. 340 beta users in the first month. Onboarding completion rate: 78% (industry average for this category: ~45%). Course completion at 30 days: 34% (vs. 15% category average — though this is early data).

The most meaningful metric: 67% of users in exit interviews said they felt "in control" of their learning, despite the AI doing significant work behind the scenes.

That's the design working.
